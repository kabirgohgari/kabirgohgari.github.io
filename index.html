Autodesk Inventor 3D Printing Arduino Team Project Cornerstone Design
Autonomous Catapult Robot
Cornerstone Design — MECH 1201 · 2026

Designed and built an automated projectile delivery system integrated onto an Arduino ELEGOO Smart Robot Car. The system was required to autonomously navigate an obstacle course, reload, and deliver three ping pong balls into a target 3 meters away — all within the width of the chassis.

I designed the snail-cam mechanism and launcher assembly in Autodesk Inventor, then coordinated 3D printing of both parts through the university's fabrication facility. The snail cam converts continuous rotary motor output into a repeatable linear compression-and-release cycle, enabling autonomous reloading without human intervention.

The project challenged our team to balance mechanical design constraints, weight distribution, center-of-gravity management, and embedded systems integration simultaneously.

AutoCAD Technical Drawing GD&T Individual Project Eng. Graphics 1102
Linear Slide Assembly
Engineering Graphics — MECH 1102 · 2025

Independently designed a complete 7-component linear slide mechanism in AutoCAD as the final project for Engineering Graphics. Every part — including the bottom plate, guide shafts, top plate, pillow blocks, PTFE bearings, and socket head cap screws — was individually modeled and fully documented.

The deliverable included a fully annotated assembly drawing with multi-view projections, a Bill of Materials specifying part numbers, quantities, and materials, and a detailed part drawing for the 10-24 UNC Socket Head Cap Screw dimensioned to 1:1 scale.

Parametric Lego Brick Model
Self-Directed — Autodesk Inventor · Grade 12

Built a fully parametric Lego brick model in Autodesk Inventor using iLogic-driven design tables. A dropdown menu allowed selection of any standard Lego brick size — the model would automatically recalculate stud count, wall geometry, internal tube placement, part number, and colour to match Lego's real-world dimensional standards.

This project introduced me to parametric design thinking — the same principle used in industrial product families and variant-based manufacturing. Understanding that a single master model can drive an entire product family is foundational to how automotive OEMs manage part variants at scale.
